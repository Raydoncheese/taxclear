Limited company tax rates

Corporation Tax on profits, dividend tax on distributions, Income Tax on salary — and VAT if registered.

⚠️ General information only. 2024/25 HMRC rates. Always verify with a qualified accountant. gov.uk →

Corporation Tax

Corporation Tax is charged on your company's profits. The rate depends on profit level — the small profits rate, main rate, and a tapered marginal rate apply in between.

Profits up to £50,000 — Small profits rate19%
£50,001 – £250,000 — Marginal (tapered)19–25%
Profits over £250,000 — Main rate25%
ℹ️
Associated companies: If you or your associates control multiple companies, the thresholds are divided between them. Two equal companies share the £250,000 threshold — each gets £125,000 max before paying main rate.
🔗 Corporation Tax rates on gov.uk →

Dividend Tax

Dividends are paid from post-Corporation-Tax profits. Each person has a £500 dividend allowance — dividends above this are taxed at the following rates.

Up to £500 — Dividend Allowance0%
Basic rate taxpayer (income up to £50,270)8.75%
Higher rate taxpayer (£50,271 – £125,140)33.75%
Additional rate (over £125,140)39.35%
💡
Dividends use your Income Tax bands — they stack on top of salary when calculating which band applies.
🔗 Dividend tax on gov.uk →

VAT

Compulsory registration once taxable turnover exceeds £90,000 in any rolling 12-month period.

Standard rate — most goods & services20%
Reduced rate — e.g. home energy5%
Zero rate — e.g. food, children's clothing0%
💡
Flat Rate Scheme: If your VAT-inclusive turnover is under £150,000, you can use HMRC's Flat Rate Scheme — pay a fixed percentage of turnover and keep any difference.

Filing Guides

What you need to file & when

Overview of the key filing obligations for limited companies.

⚠️ General information from HMRC gov.uk and Companies House. Filing obligations depend on your specific circumstances. Always verify with your accountant.
🏛
Corporation Tax Return (CT600)
Filed annually · Due 12 months after accounting period
▼

The CT600 is your company's Corporation Tax return. You must file it within 12 months of your accounting period end, even if you have no tax to pay. Pay Corporation Tax due within 9 months and 1 day of your period end.

🚨
Pay before you file. The payment deadline (9 months + 1 day) is earlier than the filing deadline (12 months).
🔗 File Corporation Tax return →
📋
Confirmation Statement (Annual Return)
Companies House · Due annually · £34 filing fee
▼

Every limited company must file a Confirmation Statement with Companies House once per year. It confirms or updates your registered address, SIC code, shareholders, and directors. Filing fee is £34 online.

⚠️
Missing this can result in compulsory strike off of your company. Set a calendar reminder.
🔗 Confirmation Statement →
📁
Annual Accounts (Companies House)
Due 9 months after year end · Publicly visible
▼

Annual accounts must be filed at Companies House within 9 months of your accounting period end. They're publicly visible. Small companies can file abridged accounts. Micro-entities have a simplified option.

Accounts must include a balance sheet and, for larger companies, a profit and loss account and directors' report.

🔗 Company accounts →
💼
Director's Self Assessment
Personal tax return · Due 31 January
▼

As a company director you must also file a personal Self Assessment return annually, reporting your salary, dividends, and any other income. Deadline is 31 January for online filing.

🚨
Dividends aren't taxed through PAYE. You declare and pay dividend tax via Self Assessment only.
🔗 Self Assessment →
🧾
VAT Return (if registered)
Quarterly via Making Tax Digital
▼

If VAT registered, you must file quarterly returns and maintain digital records under Making Tax Digital. Returns are due 1 month and 7 days after each VAT period ends.

💡
Set up a Direct Debit to HMRC for VAT payments — avoids missing the deadline.
🔗 VAT returns →
👥
PAYE & Payroll (if you pay salary)
Real Time Information — every pay period
▼

If you pay yourself or employees a salary, you must operate PAYE and submit Real Time Information (RTI) returns to HMRC on or before each payday. Even paying just the director requires PAYE registration.

Employer NI contributions are due monthly — 19th of the following month by bank transfer, or 22nd electronically.

Pensions

Director pension contributions

Company pension contributions are one of the most tax-efficient ways to extract money from a limited company.

⚠️ General information only. Sourced from gov.uk and MoneyHelper. Consult a regulated financial adviser for personal advice.
1
Employer contributions are the most tax-efficient
Corporation Tax deduction AND no NI
▼

When your company makes a pension contribution on your behalf, it is deducted from company profits before Corporation Tax — saving 19–25% CT. The contribution also has no Income Tax or NI implication for you personally.

💡
Example: A £10,000 employer pension contribution costs only £7,500–£8,100 after CT relief. The same £10,000 taken as a dividend costs £10,000 in CT plus dividend tax on top.
🔗 Pension tax relief →
2
Annual Allowance — the limit
£60,000 per year or 100% of earnings, whichever is lower
▼

The Annual Allowance is £60,000 (2024/25). This covers all contributions — employer and employee combined. If you're a sole director with a small salary, note that employee contributions are capped at 100% of your earnings — but employer contributions are not limited this way.

ℹ️
Carry forward: Unused Annual Allowance from the past 3 years can be carried forward if you were a pension scheme member.
🔗 Annual Allowance →
3
Find and consolidate old pots
Free Pension Tracing Service — over 200,000 schemes
▼

Use the government's free Pension Tracing Service to find contact details for any previous workplace or personal pension. Search at gov.uk/find-pension-contact-details.

🔗 Pension Tracing Service →
4
State Pension — check your record
35 qualifying NI years needed for the full £221.20/week
▼

As a director taking a salary above the Lower Earnings Limit (£6,396/year in 2024/25), you qualify for NI credits toward your State Pension. Check your NI record and forecast at gov.uk/check-state-pension.
